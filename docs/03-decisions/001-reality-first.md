# Decision 001 – Reality First

## Status

Accepted

---

## Decision

LifeOS exists to support real-world growth.

Gamification is used to increase motivation, awareness, and engagement, but it must never become more important than the real experiences it represents.

The purpose of LifeOS is not to create a game that users optimize.

The purpose is to help users live intentionally and learn from their experiences.

---

## Problem

Many productivity and gamification systems encourage users to optimize the system itself rather than improve their lives.

This creates unhealthy behaviors such as chasing streaks, maximizing points, or completing meaningless tasks simply because they are rewarded.

---

## Rationale

LifeOS should always reinforce real-world progress.

XP, achievements, celebrations, and analytics exist only to visualize and encourage meaningful growth.

Success is measured by improvements outside the application.

---

## Trade-offs

Pros

* Encourages sustainable growth.
* Prevents unhealthy optimization.
* Keeps the system grounded in reality.

Cons

* Less addictive than traditional gamification.
* Progress may appear slower than in heavily gamified systems.

---

## Consequences

Every future feature should support real-life experiences rather than compete with them.

Whenever a feature encourages users to optimize the system instead of themselves, it should be reconsidered.
