# LifeOS Game Mechanics

## Purpose

This document defines the core mechanics that drive LifeOS.

Game mechanics exist to support meaningful personal growth, encourage reflection, and generate useful insights. They should never distract from real-world experiences or become goals in themselves.

The mechanics described here represent the MVP and are expected to evolve as LifeOS collects data and user feedback.

---

# Core Gameplay Loop

The LifeOS gameplay loop is intentionally simple.

```text
Choose a Project
        ↓
Create a Quest
        ↓
Complete the Quest
        ↓
Log the Event
        ↓
Earn XP
        ↓
Generate Insights
        ↓
Continue or Reflect
```

The objective is not to complete as many quests as possible.

The objective is to create meaningful progress that can later be understood through analytics.

---

# Core Entities

LifeOS revolves around five primary concepts.

## Areas

Areas represent the major domains of life.

Current MVP Areas:

* Career
* Wealth
* Lifestyle
* Character

Areas provide structure but are not goals themselves.

---

## Projects

Projects represent meaningful goals with a beginning and an end.

Examples:

* Become a Data Analyst
* Build Trading Portfolio
* Host My First Dinner Party

Projects answer:

> **Why am I doing this?**

---

## Quests

Quests are small, actionable steps that move a project forward.

A quest should:

* Move a project forward.
* Be completable in one sitting.
* Have a clear finish condition.
* Produce one measurable event.
* Feel easy to start.

Examples:

* Complete one SQL exercise.
* Cook a new pasta recipe.
* Review ten losing trades.
* Meditate for one guided session.

Quests answer:

> **What should I do next?**

---

## Quest Log

Every completed quest generates one Quest Log entry.

The Quest Log is the foundation of all analytics.

It records:

* Completion date
* Time invested
* XP earned

Future versions may include additional context such as mood or energy.

---

## Campaigns (Optional)

Campaigns are temporary periods of intentional focus.

Campaigns are **optional**.

Projects can exist independently of campaigns.

Campaigns group one or more projects together for a defined period and create natural review moments.

A campaign includes:

* Name
* Start Date
* End Date (optional)
* Intensity
* Focus Projects

Examples:

* Data Analyst Transition
* Summer Cooking Challenge
* Trading Research Sprint

---

# Quest Types

Quest Types classify how progress is made.

They are used primarily for analytics rather than progression.

Current Quest Types:

* Learn
* Practice
* Build
* Experience
* Reflect

LifeOS should visualize these patterns without suggesting that every project must follow the same sequence.

---

# XP System

XP represents meaningful personal progress.

XP should never reward activity for its own sake.

The MVP uses manually assigned values.

Future versions will adapt XP using historical user data.

XP is expected to consider:

* Challenge
* Effort
* Importance

The same activity may award different XP for different users depending on their skill level and campaign focus.

---

# Difficulty

Difficulty represents the expected effort required to complete a quest.

Suggested levels:

* Easy
* Medium
* Hard
* Milestone

Difficulty influences XP but does not determine it alone.

---

# Progress

Progress is measured through completed quests and accumulated XP.

LifeOS intentionally avoids measuring success through:

* Number of tasks
* Time spent
* Daily streaks

Meaningful progress is more important than busy activity.

---

# Momentum

Momentum replaces traditional streak mechanics.

Momentum reflects recent engagement without punishing breaks.

Possible Momentum States:

* Building
* High
* Stable
* Slowing
* Recovering

Momentum should encourage users to continue rather than create guilt for missed days.

---

# Achievements

Achievements recognize meaningful milestones.

Examples:

* First Quest Completed
* First Project Completed
* 100 Career XP
* First Campaign Finished
* Hosted First Dinner
* Published First Dashboard

Achievements unlock automatically based on predefined conditions.

---

# Celebrations

Celebrations acknowledge meaningful accomplishments.

Unlike traditional rewards, celebrations reinforce intrinsic motivation.

Examples include:

* Favourite meal
* Small gift
* Day trip
* New book
* Sharing the achievement with friends

Celebrations are optional and personal.

---

# Campaign Intensity

Campaign intensity communicates personal commitment.

Suggested levels:

## Casual

Exploration without pressure.

## Focused

A meaningful priority alongside everyday life.

## Mission

A primary life objective for a defined period.

Intensity does not directly influence XP.

It exists to set personal expectations.

---

# Campaign Reviews

Every completed campaign generates a review.

Suggested review sections:

## Summary

* Campaign duration
* XP earned
* Projects completed
* Quests completed

## Insights

* Most active Area
* Most common Quest Type
* Strongest momentum period
* Biggest achievement

## Reflection

Suggested questions:

* What surprised me?
* What worked well?
* What would I change?
* What did I learn?
* Would I continue this project?

## Next Steps

* Continue Campaign
* Create New Campaign
* Archive Projects

Campaign Reviews become chapters in the user's personal growth story.

---

# Future Mechanics

The following mechanics are intentionally excluded from the MVP but may be introduced later:

* Adaptive XP
* Skill Trees
* Competencies
* AI Coaching
* Predictive Insights
* Team Campaigns
* Community Challenges
* Seasonal Events
* Inventory System
* Custom Avatars

Future mechanics should only be introduced if they support the core mission of LifeOS.

---

# Design Principles

Every mechanic should reinforce the following principles:

* Reality over gamification.
* Progress over activity.
* Momentum over perfection.
* Celebration over reward.
* Insights over statistics.
* Encouragement over pressure.
* Learning over productivity.

Whenever a proposed mechanic conflicts with these principles, the principles should take precedence.

---

# MVP Success Criteria

The MVP is considered successful if users can:

* Organize meaningful projects.
* Break projects into achievable quests.
* Log completed work with minimal effort.
* Track progress without feeling pressured.
* Complete campaigns and reflect on them.
* Generate useful insights from their own data.

LifeOS succeeds when it helps users better understand how they grow—not when it simply helps them complete more tasks.
