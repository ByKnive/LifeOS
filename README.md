# LifeOS

## Collect experiences. Produce insights.

LifeOS is a personal growth operating system designed to transform everyday progress into meaningful data.

The goal is not to create another habit tracker or productivity tool.

The goal is to build a system that helps answer:

* What activities actually move my life forward?
* What environments create my best performance?
* Which goals maintain my motivation?
* Where am I improving, and where am I stagnating?
* How can I make better decisions using data?

LifeOS combines gamification, personal development, and analytics into one evolving system.

---

# Vision

Create a system that makes personal growth engaging by turning life progress into a measurable, analyzable, and rewarding journey.

LifeOS treats personal development like a long-term game:

* Projects become quests.
* Progress becomes XP.
* Milestones become achievements.
* Experiences become data.
* Data becomes insights.

---

# Reality First Principle

LifeOS exists to enhance real-world progress, not replace it. Gamification is used to increase motivation and awareness, but the ultimate measurement is improvement outside the system.

---

# Core Concept

The LifeOS progression loop:

```
Choose a Quest
      ↓
Complete Activity
      ↓
Earn XP
      ↓
Update Progress
      ↓
Unlock Achievements
      ↓
Generate Data
      ↓
Discover Insights
      ↓
Improve Decisions
```

The system should make progress visible while continuously learning from the user's behavior.

# Core Objects

LifeOS is built around a simple hierarchy:

```
Area

 ↓

Project

 ↓

Quest

 ↓

XP

 ↓

Achievement

 ↓

Insight
```

## Areas

High-level life categories.

## Projects

Meaningful goals with a beginning and an end.

Examples:

* Become a Data Analyst
* Build an Automated Trading Strategy
* Master Italian Cuisine

## Quests

Small actionable steps that create progress.

Examples:

* Complete SQL exercise
* Analyze dataset
* Backtest trading idea
* Cook a new recipe

## XP

A measurement of progress earned through completed quests.

## Achievements

Milestones that recognize meaningful progress.

## Insights

The most important output of LifeOS.

The purpose of collecting data is not tracking for the sake of tracking.

The purpose is understanding patterns and making better decisions.

---

# Design Principles

## 1. Low friction

Tracking progress should take seconds, not minutes.

The system should never become a burden.

---

## 2. Progress over perfection

Small consistent actions compound over time.

A completed small quest is better than an unfinished perfect plan.

---

## 3. Data creates understanding

Every completed action contributes to a growing personal dataset.

This dataset can reveal:

* Productivity patterns
* Motivation patterns
* Project completion trends
* Areas needing attention
* Personal strengths

---

## 4. Build simple, evolve gradually

LifeOS will start as a minimal viable product.

Features will only be added when they improve engagement, insight generation, or usability.
